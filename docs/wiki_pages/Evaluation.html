How Your Work Is Evaluated

The final grade is an evaluation of your work in the entire course.

  • 50% comes from Assignments 1 and 2
  • 50% comes from the Final Project (Assignments A and B)

Feedback Scale

The grade comes together from the numerical evaluation of each sub-assignment. The meaning of the numbers is:

Score Meaning
0 Insufficient: minimum requirements not met
1 Sufficient: meets minimum course criteria
2 Good: solid work with clear understanding and correct application
3 Excellent: high-quality work demonstrating depth, rigor, and clarity

These numbers are intended as feedback. There is no trivial mapping between the standard danish grading scale and the feedback provided as part of the class.

Evaluation Criteria

Assignments 1 & 2

Grades are based on:

  1. Correctness and completeness of each exercise

  2. Quality of the notebook (clear structure, well-documented code, clear explanations of results)

Final Project (Assignments A & B)

The project grade reflects both technical quality and communication quality.

1. Project design

We look at whether the project makes sense as a whole. - Is the motivation clear and meaningful? - Is the dataset suitable for the question being asked? - Do the methods and conclusions actually answer the research question?

2. Explainer notebook

Your notebook should make it easy to understand what you did and why. - Is data collection and preprocessing clearly documented? - Are the methods explained in a way others can follow? - Is there real interpretation and reflection, not just outputs? - Is the notebook well structured and readable?

3. Use of Network Science

We evaluate how well you use network science tools - Are the chosen techniques appropriate for the question? - Are they applied correctly? - Is the analysis thorough, rather than just a few basic measures?

4. Use of text analysis methods

Same idea for the text analysis. - Are the methods suitable for the problem? - Are they implemented correctly? - Is the analysis deep enough to support your conclusions?

5. Website

Your project should be understandable to someone outside the course. - Are the visualizations informative and well chosen? - Are explanations clear and accessible to a broad audience? - Is the website easy to navigate and pleasant to use?

6. What makes a project excellent:

These additional aspects can lift a project from solid to outstanding: - Creativity and original thinking
- Taking on a challenging or ambitious question/dataset - Showing awareness of ethical issues (data use, bias, privacy, limitations)
